Ada Lovelace is considered one of the first female programmers and a well known mathematician. She is most known for her significant contributions to computer science through her work on Charles Babbage’s proposed Analytical Engine. This quote by Ada Lovelace originated from “Note G” which was a computer algorithm designed to calculate Bernoulli numbers using the analytical engine. The decrypted text is attributed to Ada Lovelace where she emphasizes the idea that computers cannot synthesize any new information. It can only follow the instructions and inputs of its administrator. It can use algorithms to analyze and calculate, but it can’t use human logic to brainstorm newer solutions. In other words, the advancement of technology is limited to the scope of human intelligence. This quote is very pertinent to the Information Revolution today as there seems to be an increased reliance on AI technologies to further society as a whole. There is also a great lack of education on the technology behind artificial intelligence where users believe generative AI to be synthesizing these answers rather than summarizing and analyzing resources made by humans. Her comparison of technology’s relationship with humans to the Jacquard loom is also noteworthy. The Jacquard loom famously automated the process of weaving complex patterns using punch cards. However, punch cards were stiff paper cards that had holes punched on them to act as a binary code, or set of instructions for the machine. It’s similar to computers and AI in that it requires human input to perform tasks.
